STRUCTURAL DESIGN PATTERNS

1. Facade Pattern
"Home Theater Remote"
Real-world: A home theater has 10 devices: TV, soundbar, Blu-ray, streaming box, lights, curtains, AC. 
To watch a movie, you'd need to: turn on TV → set input to HDMI1 → turn on soundbar → set to movie mode → 
dim lights → close curtains → turn on Blu-ray → insert disc... Exhausting!
A Facade provides one button: "Watch Movie" that handles everything.

2. Decorator Pattern
"Coffee Shop Add-ons"
Real-world: You order a coffee. Then you add milk (+$0.50), 
then sugar (+$0.25), then whipped cream (+$0.75). Each add-on wraps the previous drink, 
adding behavior (cost, description) without changing the original coffee class.

3. Adapter Pattern
"Universal Power Plug Converter"
Real-world: You have a European laptop charger (round pins) but you're in the USA (flat pins). 
You can't change the laptop or the wall socket. Instead, you use a plug adapter — it looks like a USA plug to the socket, 
but connects to your European charger.
