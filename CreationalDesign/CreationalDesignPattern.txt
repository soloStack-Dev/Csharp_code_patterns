CREATION DESIGN PATTERNS

1. Singleton Pattern
"One CEO, One Company"
Real-world: A hospital has one central patient database. Every doctor, nurse,
and receptionist must access the same database — not copies, not different instances.

2. Factory Pattern
"One Menu, Multiple Chefs"
Real-world: A restaurant has a single order system (the factory). 
You order "Burger" — the kitchen decides whether to make a CheeseBurger, 
VeggieBurger, or ChickenBurger based on your choice. You don't care which chef makes it.

3. Abstract Factory Pattern
"Restaurant Chain, Different Regional Styles"
Real-world: A fast-food chain operates in USA and India. Both sell "Meal Combos" (burger + drink + fries), but ingredients differ:
USA: Beef burger, Coke, regular fries
India: Paneer burger, Thums Up, masala fries
The abstract factory ensures each region creates a consistent family of products.

4. Builder Pattern
"Custom Pizza Order"
Real-world: At a pizza shop, you build your order step by step:
Choose crust (thin/thick/stuffed)
Choose sauce (tomato/bbq/white)
Add toppings (pepperoni, mushrooms, olives...)
Add extras (cheese burst, extra spicy)
The builder lets you construct complex objects step by step without a massive constructor.

5. Prototype Pattern
"Copy a Medical Report Template"
Real-world: A hospital has standard report templates (blood test, X-ray, MRI). 
Instead of creating each from scratch, the doctor clones a template and fills in 
patient-specific details. The template itself stays unchanged.
